# Project Atlas

## Who built this
Built by Kevin — 3+ years of competitive lifting, student-athlete background, and a frustration that no fitness tool actually thinks alongside a serious athlete.

## The problem
Every fitness app on the market is a data recorder. They log what happened. None of them tell you why your bench has stalled for six weeks, whether your body is ready for a PR attempt, or that you've been under-recovering since you added a second leg day. Athletes deserve a system that interprets, not just records.

## Mission
Build an intelligent fitness agent that trains alongside the user — one that understands training load, recovery patterns, and performance trends well enough to answer the questions a real coach would answer.

## What Atlas does differently
Atlas is not a tracker. It is an interpreter.

It ingests real workout data (initially sourced from Hevy export), builds a longitudinal model of performance, and surfaces insights that only become visible across months of training — plateau detection, fatigue accumulation, readiness scoring, and PR prediction.

## Core questions Atlas should answer
- "How ready am I for a PR attempt this week?"
- "Why has my squat been stalled for the past month?"
- "Am I accumulating too much fatigue to hit my goals?"
- "What should my next training block look like?"

## What Atlas is not
- Not a generic fitness app for beginners
- Not a calorie counter
- Not a replacement for a coach — a force multiplier for one

## Long-term vision
An agent that a serious lifter can have a real conversation with about their training. Not a dashboard. A thinking partner.

## Initial dataset
Seeded from Kevin's personal Hevy training logs — 3+ years of real strength training data across compound and accessory movements. This is not synthetic data. It reflects real progressive overload, real plateaus, real deload weeks, and real athletic periodization.
